The Bookshelf

My Reads

A writer who doesn’t read is like a cook who doesn’t eat. These are the books living on Tiana’s shelf right now.

I read across every genre I can get my hands on — horror, fantasy, romance, mystery, contemporary. Every book I read teaches me something about the kind of author I want to become.

● Currently Reading
Mexican Gothic
Silvia Moreno-Garcia
Mexican Gothic
★★★★★

Creeping dread done perfectly. The house itself is the villain. This is the gothic atmosphere I'm reaching for in Before Dawn.

★ All-Time Favourites
The Hunger Games
Suzanne Collins
The Hunger Games
★★★★★

The first series that taught me a story can be fast-paced AND emotionally devastating at the same time.

Coraline
Neil Gaiman
Coraline
★★★★★

Short, perfectly shaped, genuinely scary. Gaiman writes dread the way I want to one day.

Percy Jackson
Rick Riordan
Percy Jackson Series
★★★★★

The voice. The humour. The world-building hidden inside adventure. I've read these five times minimum.

A Good Girl's Guide to Murder
Holly Jackson
A Good Girl's Guide to Murder
★★★★★

I finished this in one night. The mystery structure is incredible — every chapter pays off something from three chapters earlier.

The Maze Runner
James Dashner
The Maze Runner
★★★★☆

The mystery box structure — drip-feeding information to keep you reading — is something I used directly in Before Dawn.

An Ember in the Ashes
Sabaa Tahir
An Ember in the Ashes
★★★★★

Dual POV done perfectly. Both characters are equally compelling and the world feels massive and real.

■ Recently Read
They Both Die at the End
Adam Silvera
They Both Die at the End
★★★★★

The premise tells you exactly how it ends and you read it anyway. That's craft.

The Inheritance Games
Jennifer Lynn Barnes
The Inheritance Games
★★★★☆

Genuinely addictive. Solved two of the puzzles before the reveal and was still surprised by the ending.

Legendborn
Tracy Deonn
Legendborn
★★★★★

Arthurian legend + American history + mystery. The scope is incredible and Bree is one of my favourite YA protagonists.

◆ If You Liked Before Dawn, Try These

Same fog. Same dread. Same can’t-put-it-down feeling.

Mexican Gothic
Silvia Moreno-Garcia
Mexican Gothic
★★★★★

Gothic atmosphere, creeping dread, a house that feels alive. If you loved the fog in Before Dawn, this is your next read.

Coraline
Neil Gaiman
Coraline
★★★★★

A world with rules that seem helpful until they’re not. Short, perfectly shaped, and genuinely scary.

The Merciless
Danielle Vega
The Merciless
★★★★☆

YA horror with real teeth. New girl, strange town, secrets that shouldn’t exist. Fans of Before Dawn’s atmosphere will feel right at home.

The Forest of Hands and Teeth
Carrie Ryan
The Forest of Hands and Teeth
★★★★★

An isolated community, rules that protect them, and the terrifying thing just beyond the boundary. Sound familiar?

Plain Bad Heroines
Emily M. Danforth
Plain Bad Heroines
★★★★★

Dual timeline, gothic horror, a cursed school. Rich atmosphere and mystery that layers perfectly — for fans who want more complexity after Before Dawn.

★ Anime Picks (Because They Count)

These aren’t books, but they’ve shaped my storytelling more than most books have.

Attack on Titan
Hajime Isayama
Attack on Titan
★★★★★

The unreliable narrator and the way the story recontextualises everything you thought you knew — masterclass in long-form plotting.

Your Lie in April
Naoshi Arakawa
Your Lie in April
★★★★★

Taught me that heartbreak, if earned properly, is the most powerful emotional tool a storyteller has. I cried for an hour.

Demon Slayer
Koyoharu Gotouge
Demon Slayer
★★★★★

Visual storytelling at its best. The emotional gut-punches come from character investment, not shock value. I study this constantly.
