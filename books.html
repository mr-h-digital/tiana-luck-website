Current Work-in-Progress

Before Dawn

When Yuki's car dies at midnight on a mountain road and every device she owns goes black, she's led into a town that doesn't appear on any map — a place with no internet, no mirrors, and three rules that its quiet overseer, Flora, delivers like scripture.

The last rule is the one that gets under her skin: if you hear three slow knocks after dawn... do not open the door.

As strange shadows move through the fog and her own reflection disappears in a mirror she was told not to own, Yuki begins to understand that the town's rules aren't eccentric superstitions. Something waits at the border of dawn. And it already knows her name.

Chapter 1
Welcome Home
▼

The fog started just after midnight. Yuki noticed it slowly at first. Thin streams drifting across the road beneath her headlights, curling like smoke between the trees...

She barely paid attention to it. Cape roads got fog sometimes. It wasn't strange. At least, not at first. But the farther she drove, the thicker it became. Soon the forest beside the road vanished entirely behind layers of pale gray mist...

Then the engine coughed. Once. Twice. Her heart dropped. The car jerked violently beneath her. Warning lights flashed across the dashboard all at once. The headlights dimmed. Then the engine died completely...

Then she saw the sign. WELCOME HOME.

Chapter 2
Stay Inside
▼

The house was warmer than Yuki expected. Not cozy, not welcoming, just warm. Like it had been waiting. She stepped inside slowly, the door closing behind her with a soft, final click that echoed a little too long in the narrow hallway...

There were no mirrors. Not in the hallway. Not in the living room. Not even a small one near the door. Nowhere. Yuki let out a quiet breath. Okay. That's intentional.

She plugged in her phone. The screen flickered. Then — the front camera turned on. Her reflection filled the screen, dim, grainy. The soft yellow light behind her casting long shadows across her face... Then something moved behind her.

Chapter 3
Knock, Knock, Knock
▼

Yuki didn't remember falling asleep. When she woke, it was still dark. The kind of dark that felt thin. Like something was about to break through it...

Her reflection stared back at her from the bathroom mirror. Messy hair. Tired eyes. Pale face. Real. Grounded. Then — her stomach dropped. Her reflection was gone. The mirror didn't show her. It showed someone else. Standing where she should've been. Tall. Still. Faceless.

Knock. ...Knock. ...... knock.
